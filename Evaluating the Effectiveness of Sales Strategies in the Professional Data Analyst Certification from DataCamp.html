Project Detail

This project is a key component of the Professional Data Analyst Certification from DataCamp. It involves analyzing the sales data of a new product line from Pens and Printers, a trusted provider of high-quality office products. The aim is to evaluate the effectiveness of three different sales strategies: Email, Call, and a combination of both.

The dataset consists of 15,000 entries, each representing a unique sale transaction.

Column Name Data Type Description
week int64 Week of sale, counted from product launch
sales_method object Sales method used for the customer
customer_id object Unique identifier for the customer
nb_sold int64 Number of new products sold
revenue float64 Revenue from sales, rounded to 2 decimal places
years_as_customer int64 Years the customer has been with us (since 1984)
nb_site_visits int64 Customer's website visits in the last 6 months
state object Customer's location (where orders are shipped)

The analysis process involves data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), business focus, and crafting recommendations based on the findings. The main goal of this project is to enhance the efficiency of our sales methods and optimize revenue.

Based on the analysis, the recommendation is to continue with the 'Email' sales method. This method was used to reach the most customers and generated the highest total revenue. While the 'Email + Call' method does generate the highest average revenue, it requires more time from the team and was used to reach fewer customers.

A metric that can be used by the business in the future to measure success in solving this problem is 'Average Total Revenue per Weeks'. This metric measures the average total revenue generated per weeks.
